## How Tool Calls Work

Each tool invocation counts as 1 call, regardless of:

- Which platform (Google, Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok)
- Which AI client (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Cursor, etc.)
- How much data is returned
- How long the operation takes

## What Counts as a Tool Call

**Counts:** Any tool invocation through MCP — `get_campaign_performance`, `research_keywords`, `create_search_campaign`, etc.

**Does NOT count:**
- Connecting your AI client to Adspirer
- OAuth authentication / login
- Viewing your account status
- AI assistant thinking / reasoning between tool calls
- Failed tool calls due to authentication errors

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
<Accordion title="Do unused calls roll over?">
No. Tool call limits reset monthly. Unused calls do not carry over to the next month.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Can I pay per call instead of monthly?">
Not currently. All plans are monthly or annual subscriptions with included tool call limits.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Does the same Adspirer account work across all AI clients?">
Yes. Your tool call limit is shared across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Cursor, and all other clients. One account, one limit.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="Can I create campaigns on the free plan?">
Yes. The free plan includes 15 tool calls/month. A typical Google Search campaign uses 5-6 calls, Meta uses 4-6, and TikTok uses 3-4. So you can create 2-3 campaigns per month on the free tier.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="What happens if I run out of tool calls mid-month?">
You'll see a message suggesting an upgrade. You can upgrade immediately — your new limit takes effect right away and resets your counter. Or wait for the monthly reset. For urgent needs, email abhi@adspirer.com.
</Accordion>
<Accordion title="How do I know which plan I need?">
It depends on how many platforms you use, how many campaigns you create, and how often you check performance. See the "Which Plan Do I Need?" section above. You can also try our [pricing calculator](https://www.adspirer.com/pricing).
</Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
